# AI Analysis of Indian NGOs

##  Overview
This project is part of my **AI Data Analytics Internship with InAmigos Foundation**.  
It explores a dataset of 11 leading Indian NGOs, analyzing their funding, beneficiaries, volunteers, and areas of work using **AI-powered insights from Julius AI**.

##  Dataset (Task -1)
The dataset includes details such as:
- NGO Name & Founder
- Headquarters & Year Founded
- Areas of Work (Education, Healthcare, Women Empowerment, etc.)
- Key Initiatives
- Beneficiaries Count
- Volunteer Count
- Annual Budget (in Crores)

Source: Dataset created during **Task 1** of the internship.

##  AI Analysis (Task 2)
Using Julius AI, the dataset was analyzed to uncover:
- **Funding Concentration**: Akshaya Patra Foundation accounts for ~39% of total funding.  
- **Sector Focus**: Education is the most common cause (6 NGOs), followed by Healthcare and Women Empowerment.  
- **Budget vs Age**: No clear link between NGO age and budget size — operating model matters more.  
- **Regional Insights**: Bengaluru NGOs have the highest average budget due to Akshaya Patra.  

##  Key Findings
- Median annual budget: **₹50 Cr**  
- Average annual budget: **₹92.6 Cr** (skewed by Akshaya Patra)  
- Typical NGOs operate on smaller budgets than the average suggests.  
- Education-focused NGOs reach the largest number of beneficiaries.  

## Visualizations
- Bar chart: NGO budgets

## Conclusion
AI-powered analysis reveals sharp differences in NGO scale and funding.  
Education remains the unifying cause, while healthcare and women empowerment are strong secondary themes.  
This project demonstrates how AI tools can help NGOs benchmark themselves, identify strengths, and plan better outreach.
